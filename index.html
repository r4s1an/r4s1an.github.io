// 01 — About

Background & Motivation

B.Sc. Chemical Eng.
M.Sc. Materials Eng.
A minor in Data Science

My foundation is in chemical and materials engineering, though I gravitated towards data science and software due to their potential on engineering disciplines.

During my studies, I worked on time-series forecasting of electricity consumption at NERC, where I first discovered the power of data-driven thinking in real engineering problems. For my diploma thesis, I developed a software tool to analyse the performance of perovskite solar cells at S-MAT, a project that brought my two core interests together for the first time. Since then, I have been steadily deepening my skills in programming and machine learning.

My long-term goal is a PhD at the intersection of energy chemistry and data science, applying computational methods to accelerate the development of next-generation solar materials.

// 03 — Projects

Selected Work

Data Analysis for Emerging Materials in Hybrid Perovskite Photovoltaics

An open-source Python tool that automates the analysis and reporting of current-voltage measurements from 2- and 8-pixel perovskite solar cell devices. Automatically extracts key performance parameters — fill factor, power conversion efficiency — and generates standardised PDF reports with J-V curves, performance tables, and box plots.

Python NumPy Pandas Matplotlib SciPy Seaborn Perovskite Solar Cells Photovoltaics

AI-Powered Technical Document Analysis Pipeline

An end-to-end pipeline for parsing and querying technical drilling reports. A fine-tuned DocLayout-YOLO model detects document structure, PaddleOCR and EasyOCR handle extraction, and all content is stored in PostgreSQL, queryable through a Streamlit chatbot powered by Gemini via a TAG (Table-Augmented Generation) pipeline.

Python PyTorch Streamlit PaddleOCR DocLayout-YOLO PostgreSQL LLM / Gemini Computer Vision
