// 01 — About

Background & Motivation

I have a background in chemical and materials engineering, with a focus on thin-film semiconductor systems and photovoltaic device physics. Over time, I became fascinated by how data-driven approaches can compress the materials discovery timeline from decades to years.

Today I sit at the intersection of Python-based ML/AI pipelines and electrochemical materials science — building models that predict crystal stability, band gaps, and charge transport properties before a single experiment is run.

My long-term goal is a PhD focused on computational approaches to solar cell optimization, combining density functional theory, high-throughput screening, and neural network potentials.

// 04 — Research Vision

PhD Research
Interests

Computational Discovery of Photovoltaic Materials

My research vision centres on replacing empirical trial-and-error in solar cell development with machine learning-accelerated high-throughput screening. Specifically, I am interested in training graph neural networks on DFT-calculated datasets to predict defect tolerance, charge carrier mobility, and stability under illumination — the three critical bottlenecks in perovskite commercialisation.

I am actively looking for PhD opportunities at the intersection of computational materials science and data-driven chemistry. If you are working in this space, I would be glad to connect.
